# Puzzle 63 — Haitian

Source: UKLO 2012 — https://www.uklo.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/09/2012.4-Haitian.pdf

## Context

Haitian Creole is spoken by about 10 million people in Haiti. It is based largely on French and some African languages. In the spelling, the combinations an, en, and on denote nasal vowels. The table contains nouns combined with definite articles meaning 'the'. The English translation is just for the noun itself.

## Notes
- All 12 items from Q4 converted. Items 1–8 require choosing the correct definite article form (a, an, la, lan, nan) based on the phonological properties of the noun. Items 9–12 require constructing the full noun+article form from a given English meaning.
- The solver must deduce that the article form depends on the noun's final segment and nasality patterns, and that 'yo' marks plurals.
