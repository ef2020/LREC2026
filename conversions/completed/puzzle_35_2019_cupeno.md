# Puzzle 35 — Cupeno

Source: UKLO 2019 — https://www.uklo.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/05/2019_R2_4.-Cupeno.pdf

## Context

Cupeno is an extinct language of the Uto-Aztecan family, formerly spoken in Southern California. The last native speaker, Roscinda Nolasquez, died in 1987. Here are some words in Cupeno and their translations in English:

## Match-Up Table

| # | Cupeno | Letter | English |
|---|--------|--------|---------|
| 1 | pulinchemyax | a | We were born. |
| 2 | pinehamaninqal | b | I used to embarrass him. |
| 3 | empulinwen | c | You (pl.) used to bear children. |
| 4 | imihamachemin | d | We upset [past] you (pl.). |
| 5 | chimichungpen | e | He kissed us. |
| 6 | echix | f | You (sg.) died. |
| 7 | nipulinen | g | You (sg.) assisted at my birth. |
| 8 | wixpemyax | h | They were stepped on. |
| 9 | pemhamawen | i | They used to be ashamed. |
| 10 | piwixenqal | j | You (sg.) used to step on him. |
| 11 | chungpemyax | k | They were kissed |
| 12 | pichixgal | l | He used to die |
| 13 | pimichenchixninwen | m | We used to kill them |

## Notes
- Items 11-12 are from Q4 (Cupeno to English translation).
- Items 13-14 are from Q4 (English to Cupeno translation).
- The Cupeno word for item 13 is constructed as: pimi+chem+chix+nin+wen (their+we+die+causative+used-to-plural).
- The Cupeno word for item 14 is constructed as: chimi+wix+em+in (us+step-on+you-pl+transitive).
