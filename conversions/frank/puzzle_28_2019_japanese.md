## Context

In our alphabet, letters stand for sounds. Japanese is different. Most Japanese words are written in characters called 'kanji', which the Japanese adapted from Chinese. Kanji characters represent meanings, not sounds. Here are some Japanese words in kanji with their English translations:

| Kanji | Meaning |
|-------|---------|


Known names: 山田 (Yamada) = mountain rice field; 本田 (Honda) = root rice field; 東京 (Tokyo) = east capital; 京都 (Kyoto) = capital city.
