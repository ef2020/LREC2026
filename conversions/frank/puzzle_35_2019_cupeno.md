# Puzzle 35 — Cupeno

Source: UKLO 2019 — https://www.uklo.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/05/2019_R2_4.-Cupeno.pdf

## Context

Cupeno is an extinct language of the Uto-Aztecan family, formerly spoken in Southern California. The last native speaker, Roscinda Nolasquez, died in 1987. Here are some words in Cupeno and their translations in English:

| Cupeno | English |
|--------|---------|
| pulinchemyax | We were born. |
| pinehamaninqal | I used to embarrass him. |
| empulinwen | You (pl.) used to bear children. |
| imihamachemin | We upset [past] you (pl.). |
| chimichungpen | He kissed us. |
| echix | You (sg.) died. |
| nipulinen | You (sg.) assisted at my birth. |
| wixpemyax | They were stepped on. |
| pemhamawen | They used to be ashamed. |
| piwixenqal | You (sg.) used to step on him. |

## Notes
- Items 1-2 are from Q4 (Cupeno to English translation).
- Items 3-4 are from Q4 (English to Cupeno translation).
- The Cupeno word for item 3 is constructed as: pimi+chem+chix+nin+wen (their+we+die+causative+used-to-plural).
- The Cupeno word for item 4 is constructed as: chimi+wix+em+in (us+step-on+you-pl+transitive).
