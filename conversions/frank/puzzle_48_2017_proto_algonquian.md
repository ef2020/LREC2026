# Puzzle 48 — Proto-Algonquian

Source: UKLO 2017 — https://www.uklo.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/05/2017_R2.2-Proto-Algonquian.pdf

## Context

Proto-Algonquian is an extinct reconstructed language believed to have been spoken about 3000 years ago. The ':' indicates a long vowel, and 'θ' indicates a 'th' sound as in 'thing'.

## Notes
- Context table (12 sentence pairs) and Match-Up table (3 translation items from Q2.1) combined into a single Match-Up table of 15 items.
- Q2.2 asks for grammatical explanation of affixes, which is not convertible to Match-Up format.
- The solver must deduce the morpheme system: ke-/ne- (2nd/1st person involvement), -θ- (direct 1st→2nd), -i- (inverse 2nd→1st), -a:- (3rd person object), -wa: (plural subject/object), -ena:n (plural 1st person), -aki (plural 3rd person), -ekw- (3rd→1st inverse).
