# Puzzle 37 — Lithuanian

Source: UKLO 2018 — https://www.uklo.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/05/2018_2-Lithuanian.pdf

## Context

Lithuanian is the official language of Lithuania, and is one of the surviving languages in the Baltic branch of the Indo-European family. Four Lithuanian friends -- Danute (f), Jokubas (m), Matis (m) and Regina (f) -- are planning a trip.

(Diacritics simplified; see original for precise Lithuanian orthography.)

The puzzle noted an error in the original: the tenth row originally contained "Tu turi zemelapi" instead of "Skaityk zemelapi", which was later corrected.

## Notes
- Context conversation pairs (12 items) and Match-Up translation questions (8 items) combined into a single Match-Up table of 20 items.
- Items 1-12 include speaker context information that is important for deducing verb conjugation patterns.
- Items 13-20 are from the translation questions (English to Lithuanian). The English translations include addressee context to disambiguate verb forms.
- The solver must deduce Lithuanian verb conjugation (1st/2nd/3rd person, singular/plural), negation (ne-), and question formation (Ar).
