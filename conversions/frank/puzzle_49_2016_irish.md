# Puzzle 49 — Irish (Watsonium)

Source: UKLO 2016 — https://www.uklo.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/05/2016_3.-Watsonium.pdf

## Context

Irish (Gaeilge) includes foreign loan words based on Latin, Greek or English. The Irish spelling follows pronunciation rather than original spelling. The acute accent (é) indicates a long vowel.

## Notes
- Context table (9 element name pairs) and Match-Up table (9 items from Q3.1) combined into a single Match-Up table of 18 items.
- Q3.2 (6 Irish→English guesses) not included because some items don't follow the regular rules (e.g., Iarann = iron, Luaidhe = lead) and the question explicitly notes this. Including irregular items would make the Match-Up unfair to solvers relying on the context patterns.
- The solver must deduce Irish phonetic spelling conventions: vowel lengthening with fada (é, ó, ú), consonant cluster adaptations, and the -iam/-ín suffixes.
