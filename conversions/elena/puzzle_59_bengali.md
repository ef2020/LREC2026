# Puzzle 59 — Bengali

Source: UKLO 2013 — https://www.uklo.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/09/2013.9-Bengali.pdf

## Context

Bengali (or Bangla) is the official language of Bangladesh and West Bengal, India, spoken by 230 million people. Here are some Bengali sentences (as spoken in Bangladesh) and their English translations:

| Bengali | English |
|---------|---------|
| Ami meyeţir alindaţar nice thekechi. | I've stayed under the balcony of the girl. |
| Kоbiţi gоruţir kôtha lekhe. | The poet is writing about the cow. |
| Tumi jhoŗaţa kôro. | You are making the basket. |
| Khiţi boŗolokţir gaŗiţa dekheche. | The maid has seen the car of the rich man. |
| Tumi baŗiţa theke khiţir śathe aso. | You are coming from the house together with the maid. |
| Kukurţi khêlnaţa ghôrţar modhye ane. | The dog is bringing the toy into the room. |
| Ami mondirţa theke gaŗiţa kore ghuri. | I'm traveling from the temple by car. |
| Se dinţа dhore ghurеche. | He has traveled during the day. |
| Ami khêlnaţa cheleţir śathe kori. | I'm making the toy together with the boy. |
| Коbiţi kukurţike baŗiţar baire dêkhe. | The poet is seeing the dog outside the house. |
| Guruţi boŗolokţir môto nace. | The teacher is dancing like the rich man. |
| Kobiţir dinţа eseche. | The day of the poet has come. |
| Ami cheleţike baŗiţar kache dhorechi. | I've held the boy near the house. |
| Meyeţi deśţar kôtha pôŗe. | The girl is reading about the land. |

Note: ê and ô are wider versions of e and o. ś sounds like "sh" in "shoe". ţ and ŗ are specific consonants. h after a consonant denotes aspiration. Additional vocabulary: jadukôr = "wizard", skul = "school", biŗal = "cat".
