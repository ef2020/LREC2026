# Puzzle 56 — Navajo

Source: UKLO 2014 R2 — https://www.uklo.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/2014r2.4-Navajo.pdf

## Context

Navajo is a Native American language from the Southwest United States with between 120,000 and 170,700 speakers. This problem is about the way Navajo speakers build sentences out of a verb V, its subject S and its object O. In English, SVO is almost the only possibility (e.g. *John ate porridge*), and both SOV and OVS are ungrammatical. Navajo is different, but it's still helpful to think in terms of V, S and O.

A gopher is a small American rodent, about the size of a large hamster.

## Notes
- Items 1–18 are from context sentences 1–38. Ungrammatical sentences (marked with * in the original) were excluded. Where two grammatical Navajo sentences share the same English meaning (due to two valid word orders — SOV with yi- prefix and OSV with bi- prefix), one representative was selected to maintain 1-to-1 matching. Both word orders are represented across the table so the solver can still deduce the pattern.
- Items 19–21 from Q4.1: sentences 39, 41, 42 (grammatical, with new meanings). Sentence 40 (ungrammatical, requires word-order correction) and sentence 43 (ungrammatical, ambiguous correction with two possible meanings) excluded.
