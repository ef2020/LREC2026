# Puzzle 56 — Navajo

Source: UKLO 2014 R2 — https://www.uklo.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/2014r2.4-Navajo.pdf

## Context

Navajo is a Native American language from the Southwest United States with between 120,000 and 170,700 speakers. This problem is about the way Navajo speakers build sentences out of a verb V, its subject S and its object O. Navajo word order and verb prefixes depend on a noun animacy hierarchy. The prefix 'yi-' marks SOV order and 'bi-' marks OSV order. The first noun in a sentence must be superior in the animacy hierarchy (adult > baby = large animal > small animal > insect/spider). Sentences violating these rules are ungrammatical (marked with *).
