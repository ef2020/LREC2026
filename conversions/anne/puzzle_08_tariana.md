# Puzzle 08 — Tariana

Source: UKLO 2024 — https://www.uklo.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/2024_R1_5-Tariana.pdf

## Context

Tariana is an endangered language spoken in the Vaupes river area in Brazil. There are about 100 speakers left.

Tariana has a highly developed evidentiality system. Evidentiality indicates how a speaker knows the information they are talking about. Tariana adds special endings on its verbs:

| Ending | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| -ka | the speaker saw it with their own eyes |
| -mahka | the speaker experienced it with some other sense than seeing (e.g. heard, smelled) |
| -nihka | the speaker has clues to work out what is going on (e.g. seeing wet pavement to assume rain) |
| -sika | the speaker uses what they already know (e.g. habitual knowledge) |
| -pidaka | the speaker was told by a third party |

Pronouns and verb prefixes:

| Person | Prefix | Pronoun |
|--------|--------|---------|
| 1st singular | nu- | nuha |
| 2nd singular | pi- | piha |
| 3rd singular masculine | di- | diha |
| 3rd singular feminine | du- | duha |
| 3rd plural | na- | naha |

One morning, Antonia, Candido, Margarida, Joao, Maria, and Emilio were playing a detective game about who "poisoned" their sleeping friend Ismael with manioc-flour.

**About Ismael drinking manioc-flour:**

| Speaker | Tariana Statement | English | Evidence |
|---------|------------------|---------|----------|
| Antonia | Diha kahwi-nuku diiɾaka. | He drank manioc-flour. | She saw him drink it. |
| Candido | Maye kahwi-nuku diiɾanihka. | Ismael drank manioc-flour. | He saw manioc-flour on his lip. |
| Margarida | Diha kahwi-nuku diiɾamahka. | He drank manioc-flour. | She heard him slurping. |
| Joao | Maye kahwi-nuku diiɾasika. | Ismael drank manioc-flour. | He knew he took his sister's every morning. |
| Maria | Diha kahwi-nuku diiɾapidaka. | He drank manioc-flour. | Ismael told her. |
| Emilio | Maye kahwi-nuku diiɾamahka. | Ismael drank manioc-flour. | He smelled manioc-flour on his breath. |

**About Jose playing football (his alibi):**

| Speaker | Tariana Statement | English | Evidence |
|---------|------------------|---------|----------|
| Antonia | Juse iɾida dimanikaka. | Jose was playing football. | She saw him playing. |
| Candido | Juse iɾida dimanikanihka. | Jose was playing football. | He saw kit was missing. |
| Margarida | Juse iɾida dimanikasika. | Jose was playing football. | She knew it was time for his match. |
| Joao | Naha iɾida namanikaka. | They were playing football. | He could see Jose with his brother. |
| Maria | Naha iɾida namanikapidaka. | They were playing football. | She was told by Jose's mum. |
| Emilio | Naha iɾida namanikanihka. | They were playing football. | He saw football and boots missing. |

**About what Olivia was doing (suspect):**

| Speaker | Tariana Statement | English | Evidence |
|---------|------------------|---------|----------|
| Antonia | Oli dukwasika. | Olivia was sleeping in the hammock. | She believed Olivia always sleeps all morning. |
| Margarida | Duha duayhamahka. | She was swimming. | She heard her in the river. |
| Joao | Oli kahwi-nuku dunimahka. | Olivia was making manioc-flour. | He smelled manioc-flour in kitchen. |
| Emilio | Duha hinipuku dumeyutanihka. | She was raking the garden. | He saw the garden had been raked. |

## Match-Up Table

| # | Tariana | Letter | English |
|---|---------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Duha duayhanihka. | a | She was swimming. (Candido, about Olivia — saw her clothes were wet) |
| 2 | Duha kahwi-nuku dunipidaka. | b | She was making manioc-flour. (Maria, about Olivia — told by Ismael's dad) |
| 3 | Piha | c | You (the word replacing gap (c), addressing Olivia: "You killed Ismael!") |
| 4 | Piha piayhapidaka. | d | You (Olivia) were swimming. (Candido — overheard Olivia confirm it) |

## Notes
- Converted from Q 5.1 (gaps a and b), Q 5.2 (gap c), and Q 5.3 (gaps d-i). All 9 items paired in the Match-Up table.
