# Puzzle 18 — Tseltal

Source: UKLO 2022 — https://www.uklo.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/05/9_Adv_UKLO-2022-Tseltal__Complete-Script.pdf

## Context

Tseltal is a Mayan language spoken by about 590,000 people in the Mexican state of Chiapas. Below are some Tseltal sentences about what people ate, along with their English translations. Sg. and pl. are short for singular and plural.

Key vocabulary notes: A chicken comb is the crest on top of a chicken's head. A tamale is a traditional Mesoamerican dish made of dough steamed in a corn husk or banana leaf. Panela is a block of unprocessed brown sugar. A tortilla is a thin flatbread usually made from maize. Marrow is a fatty substance found inside bones. Greens refers to green leafy vegetables.

Additional vocabulary: k'in = kidney, tsukum = stomach, chin bak = marrow, k'oxox = toasted tortillas.

Different verbs for "eat" depend on food texture: tiʔ (meats), loʔ (mushy things), k'ux (hard/crunchy things), weʔ (breads), chik' (soups/soaked things).
