# Puzzle 04 — Fur

Source: UKLO 2025 — https://www.uklo.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/2025R1-2-Fur.pdf

## Context

Fur is the language of the Fur people of Darfur, in western Sudan, and has nearly 800,000 native speakers. Below are some sentences in Fur, along with their English translations. Note that the diacritics mark high and low tone respectively; ny marks the same sound as in Spanish onion. "You" always indicates the singular pronoun.

| # | Fur | English |
|---|-----|---------|
| 1 | Kòrò jabèl. | You drink the water. |
| 2 | Yáà tòn tuumèl. | The woman builds the house. |
| 3 | Bàin kòrò lemèl. | The elder licks the water. |
| 4 | Tònà jutumèl. | You build the houses. |
| 5 | Kàrabà kòrò kelmèlà. | The animals lick the water. |
| 6 | Tòn yáà bauèl. | The house holds the woman. |
| 7 | Bàinà tòn kutumèlà. | The elders build the house. |
| 8 | Yáà dèl baèl. | The woman drinks the oil. |
| 9 | Jirgèl. | You tie. |

You are also told that the verb ruñèl means "to lift."
